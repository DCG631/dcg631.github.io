General FAQs

  • Q: What is DCG631?
  • A: DCG631 is the official DEF CON group for the 631 area code region, endorsed and recognized as part of the global DEF CON community.
    DCG631 - Suffolk County, NY
  • Q: What are DEF CON Groups (DCGs)?
  • A: The DEF CON Groups were spawned from the annual DEF CON security conference in Las Vegas. The DEF CON Groups (DCGs or DC-Groups) are a gathering point for folks interested in the alternate applications of modern technology, referred to properly as 'hacking'. DCGs are not intended to compete with any other computer groups, such as 2600, but rather to provide yet another gathering place for the discussion of technology and security topics. DCG meetings are open to anyone, regardless of their skill, age, job, gender, etc. DCGs are designed to help you learn new things, meet new people, mentor others in areas you may be strong in, and provide some cohesion within the hacker culture and it's members.
  • Q: What are the main communication channels for the group?
  • A:Our primary communication channels include email, IRC, and Discord, facilitating both real-time and asynchronous interactions among members.
  • Q: Is attending DEF CON a requirement for group members?
  • A:Attendance at DEF CON is not mandatory. Our group provides a local platform for engagement and learning, regardless of whether members attend the annual conference.
  • Q: What types of contests do you organize?
  • A:Our contests are diverse and engaging, including Capture The Flag (CTF)/Hack The Box (HTB) challenges, REGEX puzzles, Vim Golf, and Trivia nights, among others.
  • Q: How frequently are events held?
  • A:We aim to hold events regularly, typically hosting at least one event per week. These events vary from educational workshops and skill-testing challenges to casual meet-ups and contests, fostering a sense of community and continuous learning.
  • Q: Where can I find information from the previous iteration of the group?
  • A:As we integrate DCG631 into our existing structure, we're in the process of migrating and updating our content. Stay tuned for an enhanced and comprehensive repository of information.
  • Q: What topics does the group cover?
  • A:Our discussions and activities span a wide range of subjects, from core technology areas like cybersecurity, OWASP, 3D printing, reverse engineering, and IoT, to broader interests such as coding, brainstorming, gaming. Member interests play a significant role in shaping our topics.
  • Q: Can you describe the workshops?
  • A:Our workshops involve expert-led sessions where participants can learn and engage with specific topics. Past workshops have covered a variety of subjects, such as beginner guides to Vim/Nvim, Flipper Zero basics, and lockpicking.
  • Q: Are events in person only?
  • A:Events are usually held both online and in person, making them accessible to all members.
  • Q: Is there an offical meetup.com meetup?
  • Yes, https://www.meetup.com/hacktheplanet/
